AI benchmarks are broken. Here’s what we need instead.
Current AI benchmarks often fail to reflect real-world performance because they evaluate AI in isolation, detached from human teams and workflows. This leads to a gap between benchmark scores and actual utility, often resulting in AI tools being abandoned. To address this, a new approach called HAIC benchmarks (Human–AI, Context-Specific Evaluation) is proposed, focusing on team and workflow performance, long-term impacts, organizational outcomes, and system effects. HAIC benchmarks aim to provide a more realistic assessment of AI readiness for practical application. Closing this gap is crucial for responsible AI deployment and maintaining trust.
